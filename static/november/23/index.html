Tuesday, November 23 - Good Morning CMC Two quick notes:

Thank you to everyone who came to Senate last night to vote. The funding request passed by unanimous vote, so we have enough money to keep Good Morning CMC running through May.

Also I am trying to organize an ASCMC trip to go see USA Men's Soccer vs. Chile at the Home Depot Center on January 22. In the past ASCMC has lost money on trips to soccer games so they want to make sure enough people are interested. If you are thinking about not going because 'soccer is boring,' that's a really bad reason - there's a ton of other stuff that makes soccer games really fun, like singing at the top of your lungs, yelling at the ref, drinking on the bus, and seeing all of the other crazy fans. If this is something you would be willing to pay $10 to go to, sign up here.

Athenaeum

Dinner: Pauline Jones Luong. Luong is a professor of political science at Brown. She will be speaking about "The Myth of the Resource Curse." The resource curse is the idea that countries with large endowments of natural resources (oil, diamonds, etc) will get rich from selling those resources but won't ever develop the other parts of their economy.

Ath opens at 5:30; dinner starts at 6, and the program begins at 6:45.
