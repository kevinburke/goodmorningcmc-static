Events on campus

  • 5C Blood Drive. Supply levels in Southern California are currently critical. Sign up for a 15-minute block at www.givelife.org then head to the McAlister Center to donate between 10 and 4.
  • Robert Reich, former secretary of labor under Bill Clinton, speaking at the Drucker Business Forum, at the Crawford Family Forum in Pasadena, 7:45 to 11 am.
  • Ah-Eng Lai, "The Kopitiam (Coffeeshop) in Singapore: A Still-Evolving Story about Migration, Diversity and Multiculturalism." Lai is a research fellow in Singapore. Noon, Oldenborg Center (Pomona)
  • Dr. Rich Mayer, "Evidence-Based Principles for Multimedia Instruction." How to use words and pictures to promote learning, sounds pretty interesting. Followed by wine and cheese. Burkle 16 (CGU), 4-5 PM.
  • Pomona Student Union Panel on "Marriage in Contemporary America." 3 panelists who are directors of institutes or authors. 4:15pm, Edmunds Ballroom (Pomona).
  • Gretchen Edwalds-Gilbert, "Diversity of Biology majors: Many pathways to express your interests," JSD Seminar, 11-12 noon at Burns Lecture Hall (Keck Science Ctr)
  • Studio 47, a student film group at Pomona, is showing hilarious tapes from its archives. 9-11pm, Thacher Courtyard (near Pomona Art Museum)
  • Pitzer Art Gallery is opening a new exhibit, "Bas Jan Ader: Suspended Between Laughter and Tears." The artist is presumed lost at sea since 1975. Nichols Art Gallery, 5-8pm
  • Information Session, Sponsors for Educational Opportunity. SEO offers paid internships to underrepresented students of color. Heggblade Conference Room, 7-9pm.
