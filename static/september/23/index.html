Events on campus

  • "World Cup 2010: A Photo Journal," Alex Mitchell. Alex will present a series of photos from his trip to South Africa this summer. 11:45-1:00, International Place (near Heggblade).
  • Richard Haskell, "Discovering 'Scripps Green:' How it may impact climate change on campus." Haskell will speak about water use in Claremont, he's a leading sustainability researcher. Hampton Room (Scripps Dining Hall), 12:00.
  • Alexa Canady, "My Life, My Science," the first African American woman to become a neuroscientist. Not sure what the talk is about.
  • Pomona College Museum, "Art After Hours," extended museum hours with live music at 9pm from Auto De Fe and Kitchen Hips.
  • "AZ HDB 2281 and the struggle for ethnic studies," this is not the immigration bill but another bill to ban ethnic studies in Arizona high schools. Rose Hills Theater (Pomona), 6:00 pm.
  • Cupcakes, Ruth Williamson Art Gallery. Free cupcakes at 7pm.

Athenaeum

Kirby Daley, Newedge Prime Brokerage, "The Economic Outlook for Asia in the "New" Global Economic Environment." Did you know Asia is at a crossroads? Noon to 1p.m.

Mike McKay and Claudia McKay, "From the Ath to Africa, Two CMCers Share Their Decade of Experiences Abroad." The McKays used to live and work in Africa and they will tell you about it.

Ath opens at 5 for discussion; dinner is at 6; talk is at 6:45.
