Events on campus

  • Laser Tag, Parents Field, 9-12 PM. Look out for the eight men and women dressed like old school journalists.
  • Patricia Nelson Limerick, "Adventures in Applied History: Transforming Hindsight into Foresight," Rose Hills Theatre, Pomona, 11:00-12:30.
  • International Place Lunch and Conversation. CMC's Maria Martinez talks about "The Making of a Nation; The Centennial of the Mexican Revolution." International Place (near Heggblade), 11:45-1:00.
  • Lowe Institute Pizza Luncheon. Find out more about the Lowe Institute for Political Economy, or enjoy free pizza. 12:00, Founders Room, BC North.
  • Steve Grant, "The Russian Nanny: History, Culture, Mythology." Actually sounds kind of interesting. Oldenborg Dining Hall (Pomona), 12:00 PM.
  • Deloitte Consulting Information Session. 7-9 PM, Bauer Center North.
  • Deloitte Consulting "Office Hours." Not sure what those are but you should check it out if you're interested in consulting. Heggblade Conference Room, 4:00-6:00.
  • Religious Studies Welcome Back Party. For students interested in religious studies. 5:30-7PM, Stanley Patio (Pomona). Pizza and Panda Express.
  • Sheryl WuDunn, "Half the Sky: Turning Oppression into Opportunity for Women Worldwide." WuDunn is a Pulitzer Prize winning journalist and the wife of Nicholas Kristof. Garrison Theater (Scripps), 7:00 PM.
