Events on campus

  • Student Welcome. Free food, merchandise, raffle tickets and entertainment in the Claremont Village. This is marketed as a Scripps event but don't see why you can't go. 6pm-8pm.
  • Utopia in Four Movements, a live documentary. Apparently the director will be laying down the narrative live, and a musician will be providing a live soundtrack. A film will be shown as well. Sounds kinda cool actually. 5:30PM, Garrison Theater (Scripps)
  • Nuts & Bolts of Completing the Senior Thesis. Workshop at CUC. 10AM-11:30AM, Tranquada Student Services.
  • Laura Trombley (Pitzer President) Discussion and signing of her new book, "Mark Twain's Other Woman: The Hidden Story of His Final Years".11:30AM, Pitzer Founders Room.
  • Joha Louw-Potgieter, "Three Real-Life Evaluations in South Africa," the evaluations are of community, refugee and poverty programs, noon-1pm, ACB 126 (CGU).
  • Poetry Reading by Aaron Kunin and Hilary Gravendyk. Each is a Pomona Professor and has a new book. 4:15PM, Crookshank 108 (Pomona)
  • Price Waterhouse Coopers Information Session. 7-9PM, Founders Room.
  • Whitopias Rising: Race and Rage in America. Americans are verry, very angry. Rich Benjamin is the author discussing the anger. 7-9pm, Rose Hills Theater (Pomona).
  • Black Studies Graduate Program Information Session. Hosted by CGU and Intercollegiate Department of Black Studies. 6-7:30, Humanities Resource Center, 10th St.

Athenaeum

Jeffrey Bergner. Jeffrey is the president and managing financial partner of Bergner Bockorny, but before that he was a staff director of the Foreign Relations Committee and an Assistant Secretary of State for Legislative Affairs. He's talking about "Rhetoric and Reality of the Obama Administration." Ath opens at 5 for discussion; dinner is at 6; talk is at 6:45.
