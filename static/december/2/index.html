Events on campus

  • TNC, Where's Waldo, Claremont Hall 9-12:30
  • Marathon Reading of John Milton's Paradise Lost. There will be competing Heaven and Hell tables, refreshments served throughout, and apples, of course. 8AM - 8PM, Mudd Quadrangle (walk north from the library)
  • More student video screenings, featuring student videos of travel to Japan, Bali, Jamaica, Hawaii, Philippines, and China. 12pm, Oldenborg dining hall (Pomona)
  • Taco Fundraising for HB 2281 Awareness, tacos and mariachis will be sold and the funds will benefit the Save Ethnic Studies charity.
  • The 2nd night of Hanukah will be celebrated at Pitzer, with special Hanukah food, McConnell dining hall, 5:30
  • Last night to vote for the most hirsute No Shave November contestant. One vote is $1, and no-shave tanks are available for $15. 10:30, Snack
  • Blood Drive, walk ins are welcome, McKenna Auditorium, 9AM-8PM, donors will receive Cold Stone and Clippers tickets. For more info contact Mike Lapadot
  • Art After Hours at Pomona Museum of Art, poetry projects at 7pm and live music at 9pm from student bands.
  • Pomona Student Union Panel, "Bad Romance: Lady Gaga, Sexuality, and Power," featuring Pomona professors discussing feminism. 8pm, Edmunds Ballroom (Pomona)
  • "In the Works," the annual fall dance show for Pomona and Scripps dance departments, tickets are $5, 8-10pm.
