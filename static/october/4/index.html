Athenaeum

Lunch: Scott Cook P'11. Cook co-founded Intuit (who own Mint.com, Quicken, Quickbooks and TurboTax) and was an advocate of listening to your customer before Tom Peters even started talking about it. He used to watch people at Staples that bought Quicken and ask to follow them home to see how they used Quicken. Cook is a huge figure in the software industry. Lunch starts at 11:30, talk is at noon.

Dinner: Devanish Dey and Hermant Ekbote. Dey will be singing and Ekbote will be playing the tabla, with traditional Indian music to honor Gandhi's birthday.

Ath opens at 5; dinner begins at 6; lecture (open to all) begins at 6:45.
