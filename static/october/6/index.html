Events on campus

  • Writing Center Open House. Featuring desserts, prizes, custom Writing Center bookmarks, and information about the Writing Center and upcoming workshops. Bauer Center 32, 7-10PM
  • DaVita Information Session. DaVita almost went bankrupt in 1999 but had a 180 degree turnaround, and now get mentioned frequently by management gurus Bob Sutton and Jeffrey Pfeffer because of their quality of care and commitment to evidence-based management. Great place to work. Founders Room, 6-8pm
  • Heidi Latsky Dance Company performing at 5:30 in Garrison Theater (Scripps)
  • CMC-UCLA Forecast Conference. Lots of economists, government and real estate experts are gathering to discuss the future. Hosted by the Inland Empire Center, the newest on-campus institute. Citizens Business Bank Arena (in Ontario), 7:00-11:30
  • Teach for America Information Table. Applications are due by October 27th. Seal Court (outside Malott Dining Hall), Scripps, 11-1pm.

Talks

  • Mildred Garcia, "On the Margin and at the Center: Presidential Leadership for a Pluralistic Democracy." Albrecht Auditorium (CGU), 7:30PM
  • Jamie Court, "How to Raise Hell." Court is a progressive activist. Crookshank Hall (Pomona), 7-9PM
  • Jack Zipes, "Utopian Tendencies of Oddly Modern Fairy Tales," Hampton Room (Malott Dining, Scripps), noon
  • Ben Ver Steeg, "Drinking in the name of Science." He is a HMC alum and entrepreneur, presenting his new device that measures how much alcohol is in your system through tissue spectroscopy. Galileo McAlister, 4:10pm
